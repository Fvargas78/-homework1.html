Hello and welcome to my website!!
As an immigrannt, moving from one place to another,the language barrier was my first obstacle to access to a higher education in United States. Unfortunately, there's few Latinos and minorities in tech, and only 3 percent of tech execs are Hispanic. This is one of the reasons i would like to become a web developer, because in a country with a population growing more diverse each day, the U.S. tech community is monochromatic, a bastion of white, male privilege. People of color largely remain shut out of the tech industry. As a gay latino i would like to be an examples of what is possible when you wholeheartedly pursue your dreams. the other main reason i am interested in becoming a web developer is because it is a creative job. And lastly, this is a high demand job, with web technology in constant growth, it’s a pretty exciting place to be. And with countless companies on the lookout for new talent, it’s a perfect chance for aspiring developers to find their perfect role.
One person who inspire me to pursue my dreams in coding is my friend Arlen McCluskey, his website reflects all his hard world through the years. Take a look at his website: Arlen McCluskey and maybe get some tips in creating your own website.
